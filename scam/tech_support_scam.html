What is a Tech Support Scam?

Last updated: Sept 28, 2023

A tech support scam is a type of fraud in which scammers impersonate technical support representatives from reputable companies, such as Microsoft, Apple, and Google. They often contact people through pop-up messages, phone calls, or emails, claiming that their computer is infected with a virus or that they need to update their software. Once they have gained the victim's trust, they will try to convince them to pay for fake technical support services or install malware on their computer.


How do they Target you?

Tech support scams typically work in the following way:

  1. The scammer contacts the victim via phone, email, or pop-up message. The scammer may claim to be from a well-known tech company, such as Microsoft, Apple, or Norton.
  2. The scammer tells the victim that there is a problem with their computer or device, such as a virus infection or malware.
  3. The scammer offers to fix the problem for a fee. The fee may be hundreds or even thousands of dollars.
  4. The scammer asks the victim to pay the fee using a credit card, debit card, or gift card.
  5. Once the victim has paid the fee, the scammer may either disappear or provide them with fake or ineffective technical support.

Types of Tech Support Scams

Tech support scammers use a variety of methods to trick people into falling for their scams. Some common methods include:

  1. Cold Calls: Scammers may call you out of the blue, claiming to be from a tech company. They often use scare tactics, like mentioning viruses or hacking attempts on your device, to create urgency.
  2. Pop-Up Alerts: You might encounter alarming pop-up ads or messages while browsing the internet. These messages claim your computer is infected and provide a phone number for "immediate help."
  3. Unsolicited Emails: Scammers send fake emails posing as tech support, asking you to click on a link or download an attachment, which could install malware on your device.
  4. Online Ads: Fraudulent online ads offer tech support services at unrealistically low prices. Responding to these ads can lead to scams.


What Could Happen if you Fall for it?

  • Financial Loss: Scammers often demand payment for their "services," which are unnecessary or even harmful. Victims may lose money when they pay these fees.
  • Identity Theft: Giving scammers remote access to your device or sharing personal information can result in identity theft, with thieves using your data for malicious purposes.
  • Malware Infection: Clicking on links or downloading attachments from scam emails or pop-up alerts may lead to malware infections that can compromise your device's security.
  • Privacy Invasion: Granting remote access to scammers gives them full control of your computer, potentially allowing them to access sensitive files, photos, and personal information.


How can you secure yourself from Tech Support Scams?

  1. Be wary of pop-up messages and emails: Do not click on links or open attachments in messages from unknown senders.
  2. Verify Contacts:If you receive a call or pop-up message, Do not call phone numbers displayed in pop-up messages or emails. If you need to contact a tech support company, find the company's contact information on their official website.
  3. Be Skeptical: Always be cautious when unsolicited callers claim your device has problems. Legitimate tech companies won't contact you this way.
  4. Do Not Share Information: Never share personal or financial information over the phone or online with unknown tech support agents. This includes your name, address, phone number, credit card number, and Social Security number.
  5. Use Reputable Software: Install and regularly update reputable antivirus and anti-malware software to protect your device from potential threats.
  6. Educate Yourself: Learn about common scams and stay informed about the latest tactics scammers use.
  7. Report Scams: If you encounter a tech support scam, report it to your local authorities and also report to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC).

Additional Tips:

  1. Use a strong antivirus program and keep it up to date. An antivirus program can help protect your computer from viruses and malware.
  2. Do not pay for tech support services over the phone. If you need tech support, contact the company directly and ask about their payment options.
  3. Only download software from trusted sources. Be careful about downloading software from unknown websites or from peer-to-peer file sharing networks.
  4. Educate yourself about tech support scams. The more you know about tech support scams, the less likely you are to fall for one.


If you are a victim of a tech support scam

If you are a victim of a tech support scam, there are a number of things you can do:

  • Report the scam to the authorities. You can report the scam to your local police department or to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC).
  • Contact your bank or credit card company. If you gave the scammer your credit card or debit card information, contact your bank or credit card company to report the fraud.
  • Monitor your credit report for any suspicious activity. Scammers may steal your personal information and use it to commit identity theft. It is important to monitor your credit report for any suspicious activity so that you can catch it early and take steps to protect yourself.
