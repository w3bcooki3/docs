Healthcare Scams: What You Need to Know
Last updated: Sept 28, 2023

Healthcare scams involve fraudulent activities related to medical services, treatments, insurance, or products. Scammers exploit individuals' health concerns and the complexity of the healthcare system to deceive victims into providing personal information, money, or access to their medical records.

Healthcare scams can take many different forms, but some of the most common include:

  • Fake Health Products: Scammers promote ineffective or non-existent health products, supplements, or treatments, claiming they can cure various illnesses or provide miraculous results.
  • Health Insurance Scam: Scammers pose as insurance agents offering low-cost health insurance plans but require upfront payments for coverage that doesn't actually exist.
  • Medical Identity Theft: Scammers steal victims' personal and medical information to fraudulently bill for medical services, prescriptions, or equipment, leaving victims with unexpected bills.
  • COVID-19 Scams: During the pandemic, scammers offered fake COVID-19 tests, treatments, or vaccines and requested payment or personal information to exploit fears related to the virus.
  • Medical Billing Scam: Scammers call individuals claiming to represent medical providers or debt collection agencies, demanding payment for medical services that were never received.
  • Medicare/Medicaid Fraud: Scammers target the elderly or vulnerable individuals by falsely offering assistance with Medicare or Medicaid enrollment, using stolen information to commit fraud.
  • Clinical Trial Scam: Scammers pose as researchers conducting clinical trials, requesting upfront fees or personal information from participants who think they're signing up for legitimate medical studies.
  • Telemedicine Scam: Scammers set up fake telemedicine websites or services, offering remote medical consultations and prescriptions without proper medical oversight.

How people fall for healthcare scams?

People fall for healthcare scams for a variety of reasons. Some people are simply unaware of the types of scams that exist. Others may be vulnerable to scams because they are desperate for a cure for a serious illness or because they are financially struggling. Scammers are also very good at manipulating people, and they may use a variety of techniques to gain people's trust.

Tricks and techniques scammers use

Scammers use a variety of tricks and techniques to defraud people. Some common scams include:

  • Phishing: Scammers may send phishing emails or text messages that appear to be from a legitimate healthcare organization. The emails or text messages may ask for personal information, such as Social Security numbers or credit card numbers.
  • Telemarketing: Scammers may call people and claim to be from a legitimate healthcare organization. They may offer free medical tests or treatments, or they may ask for personal information.
  • Door-to-door sales: Scammers may come to people's homes and claim to be from a legitimate healthcare organization. They may try to sell fake or counterfeit medications, or they may ask for personal information.
  • Social media: Scammers may use social media to advertise fake or counterfeit medications or treatments. They may also use social media to collect personal information from people.


Real-Life Examples:

  • Example 1: Scammers offer a "miracle cure" for a serious illness, claiming it's a groundbreaking treatment. They charge exorbitant fees for the supposed remedy, which turns out to be ineffective.
  • Example 2: A scammer poses as an insurance agent, selling affordable health insurance policies to vulnerable individuals. Victims pay upfront premiums but receive no actual coverage.
  • Example 3: Scammers contact elderly individuals, offering to help them with Medicare enrollment. They steal personal information and make false claims, resulting in fraudulent charges on the victim's Medicare account.


Prevention Tips:

  1. Verify Providers: Research healthcare providers and products before purchasing or sharing personal information. Confirm their legitimacy through official websites or trusted sources.
  2. Be Cautious of Unsolicited Offers: Beware of unsolicited calls, emails, or messages offering healthcare services or products, especially if they pressure you for immediate payment or personal information.
  3. Question Miracle Cures: Be skeptical of health products claiming miraculous results or "secret" treatments. Consult with healthcare professionals for advice.
  4. Check Insurance: Verify the authenticity of insurance offers and policies through legitimate insurance companies and government sources.
  5. Report Suspicious Activity: If you suspect a healthcare scam, report it to relevant authorities, such as the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) or local law enforcement.

Additional Tips:

  1. Be aware of the latest healthcare scams. Scammers are constantly developing new scams, so it is important to stay informed about the latest trends. You can find information about healthcare scams on the websites of the FTC and other consumer protection agencies.
  2. Talk to your family and friends about healthcare scams. It is important to raise awareness about healthcare scams so that others can protect themselves from falling victim to these scams.
  3. Be vigilant. Healthcare scams can be very convincing, so it is important to be vigilant and to be suspicious of any offer that seems too good to be true.

If you are a victim of a healthcare scam

If you are a victim of a healthcare scam, there are a number of things you can do:

  1. Report the scam to the authorities. You can report the scam to your local police department or to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC).
  2. Contact your insurance company. If the scam involved your insurance company, contact them to report the fraud.
  3. Monitor your credit report for any suspicious activity. Scammers may steal your personal information and use it to commit identity theft. It is important to monitor your credit report for any suspicious activity so that you can catch it early and take steps to protect yourself.

Tip

Healthcare scams prey on people's vulnerabilities and concerns for their well-being. Stay informed, verify offers, and protect your personal information to avoid falling victim to these scams.
