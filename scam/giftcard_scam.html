Protect Yourself from Gift Card Scammers
Last updated: Sept 28, 2023

Gift card scams are deceptive schemes where fraudsters trick individuals into purchasing gift cards and providing the card information, including the card numbers and PINs, usually over the phone or online. Scammers often pose as authorities, relatives in distress, or tech support agents, and they use various tactics to manipulate victims into believing that they owe money, have a family emergency, or need to resolve an issue urgently. The scammers then demand payment in the form of gift cards, claiming it's the quickest or only way to resolve the situation. Once the victim provides the gift card details, the scammers swiftly drain the card's value, leaving the victim with financial loss and no means to trace or recover the funds.

Gift card scams can take many different forms, but some of the most common include:

  • Phishing scams: Scammers may send phishing emails or text messages that appear to be from a legitimate company, such as a bank, credit card company, or government agency. The emails or text messages may ask the recipient to purchase a gift card and then provide the gift card information to the scammer.
  • Tech support scams: Scammers may call people and claim to be from a tech support company. The scammers may convince the person to purchase a gift card and then provide the gift card information to the scammer in order to pay for tech support services.
  • Fake customer support scams: Scammers may call people and claim to be from a customer support department for a well-known company. The scammers may convince the person to purchase a gift card in order to resolve a customer service issue.
  • Fake prize scams: Scammers may call people and tell them that they have won a prize, such as a lottery or sweepstakes. However, the scammers will tell the person that they need to pay a processing fee in order to claim the prize. The scammers will then ask the person to purchase a gift card and then provide the gift card information to the scammer in order to pay the processing fee.
  • Blackmail scams: Scammers may blackmail people by threatening to release embarrassing or sensitive information about them. The scammers will then ask the person to purchase a gift card and then provide the gift card information to the scammer in order to prevent them from releasing the information.

How gift card scams work?

There are a number of steps that people can take to protect themselves from employment scams:

  1. The scammer contacts the victim and tries to gain their trust. The scammer may pose as a legitimate company or individual, such as a bank representative, tech support agent, or customer service representative.
  2. The scammer convinces the victim to purchase a gift card. The scammer may tell the victim that they need to purchase a gift card in order to pay for a product or service, to resolve a customer service issue, or to claim a prize.
  3. The scammer asks the victim to provide them with the gift card information. The scammer may ask the victim for the gift card number, expiration date, and PIN number.
  4. The scammer uses the gift card information to make fraudulent purchases or to withdraw cash.

Why gift card scams are so effective?

Gift card scams are so effective because they are difficult to track and trace. Once the victim provides the scammer with the gift card information, the scammer can use the gift card immediately to make purchases or to withdraw cash. The victim may not realize that they have been scammed until they try to use the gift card and discover that it has already been used.

Prevention Tips:

There are a number of steps that people can take to protect themselves from gift card scams:

  • Be wary of unsolicited contact from companies or individuals. If you receive a call, email, or text message from someone claiming to be from a company or individual that you do not know, be skeptical.
  • Do not purchase a gift card in order to pay for a product or service, to resolve a customer service issue, or to claim a prize. Legitimate companies and individuals will not ask you to purchase a gift card for any of these reasons.
  • Do not give out your gift card information to anyone. Your gift card information is confidential and you should only give it to people that you trust.

If you are a victim of an employment scam

If you are a victim of a gift card scam, there are a number of things you can do:

  • Report the scam to the authorities. You can report the scam to your local police department or to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC).
  • Contact the company that issued the gift card. If you purchased the gift card from a store, contact the store. If you purchased the gift card online, contact the website where you purchased it.
  • Monitor your credit report for any suspicious activity. Scammers may steal your personal information and use it to commit identity theft. It is important to monitor your credit report for any suspicious activity so that you can catch it early and take steps to protect yourself.

Additional Tips:

If you are a victim of a gift card scam, there are a number of things you can do:

  • Be aware of the latest gift card scams. Scammers are constantly developing new scams, so it is important to stay informed about the latest trends. You can find information about gift card scams on the websites of the FTC and other consumer protection agencies.
  • Talk to your family and friends about gift card scams. It is important to raise awareness about gift card scams so that others can protect themselves from falling victim to these scams

Tip

Gift card scams exploit victims' trust, fear, or emotions, and they often target vulnerable individuals. It's crucial to be aware of these scams and understand that legitimate entities, such as government agencies, utility companies, or tech support, will never demand payment in the form of gift cards. Staying informed and cautious is essential in protecting yourself and your finances from falling victim to gift card scams.
