Safeguard Your Job Search: Employment Scams
Last updated: Sept 28, 2023

Employment scams are fraudulent schemes that target job seekers by offering fake job opportunities, often promising high salaries, flexible work arrangements, or enticing benefits. Scammers aim to deceive victims into providing personal information, paying upfront fees, or performing unpaid work, ultimately exploiting their need for employment. These scams can occur through various channels, including online job boards, social media, and email, and they can result in financial loss and identity theft for victims.

  • Fake job postings: Scammers may post fake job postings on job boards or websites. These job postings may seem like legitimate job opportunities, but they are actually scams.
  • Advance fee scams: Scammers may require job seekers to pay an advance fee for things like job training, background checks, or processing fees. However, once the job seeker pays the fee, the scammer disappears.
  • Overpayment scams: Scammers may offer job seekers a job and then send them a check for more money than the job pays. The scammer will then ask the job seeker to wire the extra money back to them. However, the check will bounce and the job seeker will be left out of the money they wired.
  • Work-from-home scams: Scammers may offer job seekers work-from-home jobs that promise high pay and little work. However, these jobs are often scams and the job seeker will not actually be paid.
  • Pyramid schemes: Pyramid schemes are scams that promise job seekers high commissions for recruiting other people to the job. However, pyramid schemes are unsustainable and the job seeker will eventually lose money.

Real-life examples of employment scams

  • In 2023, a group of scammers was arrested for posting fake job postings on job boards. The scammers required job seekers to pay an advance fee for job training. However, once the job seeker paid the fee, the scammers disappeared.
  • In 2022, a scammer offered a job seeker a job as a mystery shopper. The scammer sent the job seeker a check for more money than the job paid and asked the job seeker to wire the extra money back to them. The check bounced and the job seeker was left out of the money they wired.
  • In 2021, a scammer offered a job seeker a work-from-home job as a data entry clerk. The job promised high pay and little work. However, the job seeker was never actually paid.
  • In 2020, a scammer offered a job seeker a job in a pyramid scheme. The job promised high commissions for recruiting other people to the job. However, the pyramid scheme was unsustainable and the job seeker eventually lost money.

Prevention Tips:

There are a number of steps that people can take to protect themselves from employment scams:

  • Be careful about job postings that seem too good to be true. If a job posting promises high pay and little work, it is probably a scam.
  • Do not pay any advance fees for job training, background checks, or processing fees. Legitimate employers will not require job seekers to pay any advance fees.
  • Be wary of jobs that require you to wire money to the employer. Legitimate employers will not ask job seekers to wire money to them.
  • Research any company before you apply for a job with them. Make sure that the company is legitimate and that the job posting is real.
  • Be careful about personal information that you share with potential employers. Only share personal information with employers that you trust.

If you are a victim of an employment scam

  • Report the scam to the authorities. You can report the scam to your local police department or to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC).
  • Contact your bank or credit card company. If you wired money to the scammer, contact your bank or credit card company to see if they can help you get your money back.
  • Monitor your credit report for any suspicious activity. Scammers may steal your personal information and use it to commit identity theft. It is important to monitor your credit report for any suspicious activity so that you can catch it early and take steps to protect yourself.


Tip

Job seekers must be vigilant during their job search to avoid falling victim to employment scams. By staying informed and following best practices, you can protect yourself from fraudulent job offers and maintain a safe and successful job search experience.
