What Are Romance Scams?
Last updated: Sept 28, 2023

Romance scams are fraudulent schemes where individuals, often operating under fake identities, build fake romantic relationships with victims to exploit their emotions and trust. The goal is to manipulate victims into sending money, sharing personal information, or performing other actions for the scammer's financial gain.


How Romance Scams Work?

  • Online Dating Scams: Scammers create fake profiles on dating websites, gaining victims' trust and often professing love before requesting money or personal information.
  • Military Romance Scams: Scammers pretend to be deployed military personnel, forging emotional connections with victims and seeking financial support.
  • Investment/Financial Romance Scams: Scammers pose as wealthy individuals looking for love, then trick victims into investing in fake ventures or lending them money.
  • Inheritance Scams: Scammers claim they have a substantial inheritance that they can't access and ask victims for financial assistance to release the funds.
  • Medical Emergency Scams: Scammers fabricate medical crises, seeking financial help from victims for supposed treatments or surgeries.
  • Long-Distance Relationship Scams: Scammers use distance as an excuse to avoid meeting in person, prolonging the online-only relationship while requesting financial support.
  • Catfishing Scams: Impersonating others online, scammers deceive victims into forming emotional connections, often leading to requests for money or personal information.

Real-Life Examples:

  • Example 1: A scammer poses as a military officer deployed overseas. They develop a relationship with the victim, claiming they need funds to visit or handle emergencies, convincing the victim to send money.
  • Example 2: Using a fake online dating profile, a scammer builds trust with the victim, then claims to have a sick family member who requires expensive medical treatment. They ask the victim for financial assistance.
  • Example 3: A scammer poses as a wealthy businessperson seeking love online. After gaining the victim's trust and affection, they ask for investments in a fabricated business opportunity, disappearing with the victim's money.


Prevention Tips:

  1. Stay Cautious Online: Be skeptical of strangers online, especially those who rush into romantic relationships.
  2. Verify Identities: Request proof of identity or video calls to confirm the person's real appearance and location.
  3. Avoid Sending Money: Never send money or personal information to someone you've only met online, regardless of their story.
  4. Research Profiles: Google reverse image search can help verify if profile pictures are stolen from elsewhere on the internet.
  5. Report Suspicious Activity: If you suspect a romance scam, report it to the dating website or app and local authorities.

Tip

Romance scams exploit emotions and trust. Be vigilant, take your time in developing online relationships, and remember that genuine love doesn't come with financial strings attached.
