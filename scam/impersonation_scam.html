What Are Impersonation Scam?

Last updated: Sept 28, 2023

Impersonation scams occur when fraudsters pose as trusted individuals or authorities to deceive victims. They manipulate people into taking actions they wouldn't otherwise take, such as sharing personal information or sending money.


How do Impersonation Scams work?

  1. Cold Calls: Scammers may call pretending to be government officials, bank employees, or law enforcement officers, using intimidation or fake threats to pressure victims.
  2. Online Impersonation: Impersonators create fake profiles on social media or dating websites, building trust with victims and exploiting their emotions for financial gain.
  3. Email Phishing: Scammers send deceptive emails appearing to be from reputable organizations, requesting personal information or payment for fake services.
  4. Impersonating Family or Friends: Some scams involve impersonating a victim's family member or friend, claiming to be in trouble and urgently needing money.

What Could Happen if you Fall for it?

  • Financial Loss: Victims may lose money by paying fake fines, fees, or sending money to scammers under false pretenses.
  • Identity Theft: Sharing personal information can lead to identity theft, where scammers use your data for fraudulent activities.
  • Emotional Distress: Impersonation scams can cause significant emotional stress, especially in cases where victims believe they are helping loved ones in need.
  • Compromised Security: Providing access or information to impersonators can lead to compromised computer security or social media accounts.


Prevention Tips:

  1. Verify Identities: Always verify the identity of callers, especially if they request personal information or payment.
  2. Be Cautious Online: Be wary of online profiles and requests from unknown individuals, especially on social media and dating platforms.
  3. Think Before You Act: Don't rush into providing money or information, even if the situation seems urgent.
  4. Independently Confirm: If contacted by someone claiming to be a family member or friend in distress, verify their situation through a separate and trusted means of communication.
  5. Report Suspected Scams: If you suspect an impersonation scam, report it to relevant authorities or organizations.

Note

Impersonation scams prey on trust and emotions. Stay vigilant and use caution when dealing with unsolicited requests for personal information or money.
