RansomwareLast updated: 2019-06-01

It is a type of malicious software that encrypts a victim's data and demands a ransom payment in exchange for restoring access to the encrypted files. Different ransomware incidents can vary based on their targets, methods of infection, and impact.


Here is how you can get infected with Ransomware:

  • Phishing Emails: Clicking on links or opening attachments in deceptive emails that pretend to be from trustworthy sources can lead to ransomware infection.
  • Fake Websites: Visiting fake or compromised websites, especially after clicking on suspicious links, may trigger ransomware downloads.
  • Malicious Ads: Clicking on ads on unfamiliar or untrusted websites can inadvertently download ransomware.
  • Software Downloads: Downloading software from unverified sources or unofficial websites can expose users to ransomware.
  • Infected External Devices: Connecting infected USB drives or external devices to your computer can spread ransomware.
  • Fake Software Updates: Downloading updates from untrusted pop-ups or unverified sources can lead to ransomware installation.
  • Social Engineering: Falling for scams where someone pretends to be a trusted person or organization, urging you to click or download something.
  • Outdated Software: Not keeping your operating system and software up to date can leave vulnerabilities for ransomware to exploit.
  • Weak Passwords: Using weak or easily guessable passwords can make it easier for cybercriminals to gain access to your system.
  • Not Backing Up Data: Not regularly backing up important files means you have no copy to restore if ransomware encrypts your data.
  • Opening Infected Documents: Opening files from unknown sources or unexpected emails might trigger ransomware.
  • Using Cracked Software: Downloading pirated or cracked software exposes users to the risk of ransomware.
  • Ignoring Security Warnings: Ignoring or dismissing security warnings on your computer can leave you vulnerable to ransomware attacks.
  • Unsecured Remote Access: Allowing unauthorized access to your computer through remote desktop services can lead to ransomware infections.
  • Unprotected Mobile Devices: Not securing your smartphones and tablets can make them susceptible to ransomware.

Prevention Tips

  • Be Cautious with Emails: Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unsolicited or suspicious emails. Verify the sender's identity before taking any action.
  • Use Reputable Sources for Downloads: Only download software and files from trusted and official sources. Avoid downloading cracked or pirated software.
  • Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your operating system, applications, and antivirus software to patch vulnerabilities.
  • Enable Pop-up Blockers: Enable pop-up blockers on your web browser to prevent malicious pop-ups that could trigger ransomware downloads.
  • Backup Regularly: Regularly back up your important files to an external hard drive or a secure cloud service. Disconnect the backup device after the backup is complete.
  • Use Strong Passwords: Create strong and unique passwords for all your online accounts and enable multi-factor authentication whenever possible.
  • Avoid Risky Websites: Avoid visiting suspicious or untrustworthy websites. Stick to reputable websites for online shopping and transactions.
  • Educate Yourself: Stay informed about the latest ransomware threats and tactics to recognize potential risks.
  • Be Wary of Social Engineering: Be cautious of unsolicited phone calls or messages asking for personal information or payment details.
  • Use Antivirus and Anti-Malware Software: Install reputable antivirus and anti-malware software on your computer and keep it updated.
  • Be Skeptical of Urgency and Threats: Beware of emails or messages that create a sense of urgency or threaten negative consequences if you don't take immediate action.
  • Disable Macros: Disable macros in documents and files, as they can be used to deliver ransomware.
  • Secure Your Mobile Devices: Use passcodes or biometric authentication on your smartphones and tablets to protect against unauthorized access.
  • Don't Pay Ransom: If you become a victim of ransomware, never pay the ransom. Paying does not guarantee recovery and encourages cybercriminals to continue their attacks.
  • Report Suspicious Activity: If you encounter any suspicious emails or websites, report them to your email provider or the appropriate authorities.
