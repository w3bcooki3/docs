Phishing

Fake Financial
Account
Compromise
Email

Arrest Threat

Scammers call victims, claiming that they have outstanding warrants or pending legal issues, and demand immediate payment to avoid arrest.

Family Member Arrest

Scammers impersonate law enforcement officers, saying that a family member has been arrested, and they need money for bail or legal fees.

Kidnapping Threat

Scammers claim that a family member has been kidnapped, using distressing details to demand ransom money from the victim.

Fake Fines

Scammers allege that the victim owes fines or fees for various reasons, such as missing jury duty or not paying taxes, and threaten legal consequences if payment isn't made promptly.
Scam & Fraud

Romance
Scam

Online Dating Scam

Fraudsters create fake profiles on dating websites to establish emotional connections with victims, often leading to requests for money or personal information.

Military Romance Scam

Scammers pretend to be members of the military, using fake stories to exploit emotions and solicit money or gifts from their targets.

Long-Distance Relationship Scams

Scammers create distance as an excuse for not meeting in person, using various tactics to prolong the online-only relationship while seeking financial support.

Catfishing Scams

Perpetrators create false identities to establish online relationships, often using fake photos and stories to deceive victims into forming emotional connections
Scam & Fraud

Healthcare
Scam

Fake Health Products

Scammers promote ineffective or non-existent health products, supplements, or treatments, claiming they can cure various illnesses or provide miraculous results.

Health Insurance Scam

Scammers pose as insurance agents offering low-cost health insurance plans but require upfront payments for coverage that doesn't actually exist.

Medical Identity Theft

Scammers steal victims' personal and medical information to fraudulently bill for medical services, prescriptions, or equipment, leaving victims with unexpected bills.

Medical Billing Scam

Scammers call individuals claiming to represent medical providers or debt collection agencies, demanding payment for medical services that were never received.

Medicare/Medicaid Fraud

Scammers pose as researchers conducting clinical trials, requesting upfront fees or personal information from participants who think they're signing up for legitimate medical studies.

Impersonation of Legitimate Companies

Scammers set up websites, phone lines, and email addresses that closely resemble those of legitimate tech companies, making it difficult for victims to distinguish between real and fake support.

Telemedicine Scam

Scammers set up fake telemedicine websites or services, offering remote medical consultations and prescriptions without proper medical oversight.
Scam & Fraud

Employment
Scam

Job Offer Scam

Scammers post fake job listings online, promising high pay and great benefits to lure job seekers. They may ask for upfront fees or personal information.

Work-From-Home Scam

Fraudsters advertise remote work opportunities that seem convenient. However, they might ask for money to provide starter kits or training materials that never materialize.

Interview Scam

Scammers contact individuals with a fake job interview, aiming to gather sensitive information like social security numbers or bank details under the guise of employment requirements.

Overpayment Scam

In this scam, victims receive a fake job offer and a large check. They're asked to deposit the check and send back a portion, only to find out later that the check bounces.
